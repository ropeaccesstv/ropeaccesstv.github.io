1. Having Few Business Contacts

Sounds easy, but itâ€™s true. Contacts are the glue of rope access, like any other job: if you don't have business contacts, who will call you to work? And if you have few contacts (or worse, only one), what do you do when your only contact suddenly closes the business or turns to some other self-employed rope access worker?

Easy: make new contacts. Whether you are at work, in the supermarket, at the doctor, at a barbecue with the neighbors, be friendly and sociable, and find out if the person you are talking to knows someone who can help finding new customers.

In the best case, you will have created a new business contact.

Worst case scenario, the person you spoke to will tell someone else about you and spread the word for you. In no time you will receive calls and messages from customers looking for a self-employed worker who can solve their rope access problem.

Remember: a net catches fish only if the links are well sewn together.

2. Accept Jobs Beyond your Ability

How many times did you accept a challenge that you knew you couldn't win?

In the rope access industry, taking this kind of risks can seriously endanger your life and also the life of others.

There is nothing wrong with admitting that some kind of rope access job is beyond your ability: if you are a self-employed worker and a customer offers you a construction site that requires months of time and dozens of workers, it would be crazy to accept it, other than potentially dangerous.


If you admit that you have limits, this does not make you ridiculous in the eyes of the customer, but you demonstrate that you have a great sense of responsibility and seriousness, extremely important qualities in this category of work (and, in general, in life).

4. Lowering your Price to get Hired Faster

The trend of bringing down market prices in order to getting your hands on a certain job is not good for anyone. It creates damage not only to self-employed workers, but also to companies (as well as making the rope access business to lose credibility).

So when the customer offers you a job and you are afraid of offering an high price, think carefully before accepting it for a few cents.

Rope access is a serious matter: get paid the right price for your performance.
