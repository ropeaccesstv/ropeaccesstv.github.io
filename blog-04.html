1. It’s Normal to be Scared.

When I started working in the rope access industry, nobody ever told me that I shouldn't be afraid. I always thought it was implied that courage was an essential skill for this profession.

That was a mistake.

Fear is normal, it will always stay with you. If you get rid of fear, you can seriously endanger your life.

After all, fear is what prevents you from doing very dangerous things.

When you start working in the rope access world, you never feel safe. You are high, very high, for many hours a day.

Only when you return to the ground the feeling of fear will slowly fade away.

After this initial phase, where you perceive a series of small improvements every day, you begin to feel more and more comfortable, finally finding your comfort zone.

And everything becomes clear. You can concentrate on the work you are doing, forgetting the height, the fears, the feeling that something can go wrong.

2. It Makes You Grow.

Working as a rope access operator, sometimes I found myself in extreme situations from which I could get out relying only on my own strength. To put it simple, in this business you can quickly end up in uncomfortable and dangerous situations.

When you work in rope access you are high, so high that no ladder can reach you. If you have an unexpected situation, only a colleague of yours, a technician like you, will be able to help you. Soon you understand that relying on others is fundamental, it is part of teamwork.

But learning to help yourself is essential, taking advantage of the analytical mind, the strength of your muscles, the courage you have developed over the years.

After resolving the problem that blocked your path, you realize that you have learned something. Somehow, you have grown, inside and outside. In the mind, as well as in the body. You have become a better person.

3. There’s high demand.

Rope access is a niche job. Few do it, because most people are afraid of heights. This means that there will be many more job listings.

Also consider this: with the continuous technological and construction development in the world, there will be an increase in demand for rope access works. We will have more skyscrapers, more tall buildings that are difficult to reach.

The maintenance and inspection, necessary for all buildings, will have an increase in demand in the immediate future. If you are a rope access technician, you will be part of this global change.

4. Dumbness exists, and it’s closer than you think.

As a child I believed that the adults were intelligent, honest people, who did their job with joy and happiness.

Then I grew up, and found out that wasn’t true.

The world is full of fools, unfortunately. Obviously not all of them are fools, otherwise we would have a dysfunctional, imperfect society.

The rope access business, being part of human society, includes many incompetent people, who should have stayed away from ropes and harnesses.

Many times I wondered why B., a technician I worked with in the past, chose to be a rope access technician. Why did they hire him, I wondered, since he was slower than me, more incapable and clearly unsuitable for the role?

Always expect an idiot on your way, because, as my grandfather said, "there is a fool born every minute".

5. Your Boss is a Businessman.

You have found a new job in the rope access business. You enter the company headquarters, you have a meeting with the CEO and you start talking to him.

He is a nice, sociable type, so he immediately puts you at ease.

At this point, proud of your rope access technical knowledge, you ask him what he thinks of the new high-end harness just released on the rope access market.

The CEO looks at you without understanding. Then, very casually, he changes the subject of the conversation, hoping you didn’t notice it.

You have found a new job, true.

But if you believe you have found a new friend with whom you can discuss of ropes, knots and carabiners, you are very wrong.

Except for rare cases, in most rope access companies the CEO is a merchant, not a technician. With him you will discuss about work to be done, money to be received, unpaid bills. Never about technical stuff.

Put your heart in peace: your boss will never understand the effort of being hours under the sun hanging on a rope. Because it is likely that he has never been there.

6. The Team becomes Family.

The best thing about rope access is that the people who are closest to you at work, your team of colleagues, become your second family.

Spending many hours a day with people like you, far away from everyday problems, creates strong and lasting bonds.

The sharing of time and space makes people more human and close to each other.

In a few words, the height that separates you from non-technicians can generate friendships that will last a lifetime.

7. You Become a Citizen of the World.

I have done many jobs in my life.

But in none of these I have met so many people from different cultures and countries as in rope access. If you work in a big city (London, in my case), expect to find technicians from every country of the world.

Working in a multiethnic team teaches you to respect and embrace the cultures of other people, discovering that after all there are other ways of living outside of yours. What better way to wipe away the prejudices and racism that still damage our society?
